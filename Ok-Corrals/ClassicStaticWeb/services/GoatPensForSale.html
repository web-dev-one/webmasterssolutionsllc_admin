Why Choose Our Goat Pens?

At OK Corrals, we understand that goats are clever, agile, and persistent animals that require specialized enclosures. Our goat pens are designed with these unique challenges in mind, offering:

  • Predator-proof construction to protect against coyotes, dogs, and other threats
  • Heavy-duty materials that withstand chewing, rubbing, and head-butting
  • Custom configurations for dairy goats, meat goats, or pet goats
  • Optional shade structures to protect from Arizona's intense sun
  • Secure gates and latches that goats can't manipulate
  • Easy cleaning designs with removable panels or sloped floors

Goat Pen Construction Details

Our goat pens are built with the same quality materials we use for our heavyweight horse corrals, adapted for goat-specific needs:

Materials We Use:

  • Galvanized steel panels - Rust-resistant and chew-proof
  • Schedule 40 pipe frames - For permanent installations

Frequently Asked Questions

What size pen do I need for my goats?

As a general rule, allow 15-25 square feet per goat in the pen, plus additional space for shelters and feeders. Dairy goats typically need more space than meat breeds. Our consultants can help you determine the ideal size during your free consultation.

How do you predator-proof goat pens?

We use several techniques learned from our mare motel designs: buried perimeter fencing, overhangs to prevent climbing, and small mesh sizes that prevent predators from reaching through. We can also install electric fence options.

Can you build goat pens on slopes?

Absolutely! Arizona's terrain varies greatly, and we've installed pens on everything from flat desert to steep hillsides. We use terracing techniques and custom panel sizing to accommodate any slope.

Do you offer financing?

Yes! We work with several agricultural lenders to help spread out payments for larger projects. Ask about our financing options when you call us.
